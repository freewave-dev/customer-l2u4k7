Estate Planning

A good estate plan keeps decisions in your hands and spares your family guesswork. On the island, that often means planning around a family property — who keeps it, how it’s shared, and how to avoid a forced sale.

We prepare plans in plain language, explain every document before you sign it, and keep things current as life changes.

How we can help

  • Wills
  • Revocable living trusts
  • Durable powers of attorney
  • Health care directives
  • Community property agreements
  • Guardianship nominations

When a loved one has died and an estate needs to be settled, see our Probate practice.

What to expect

  1. Goals — a conversation about your family, your property, and what you want to happen.
  2. Draft — documents in plain English, reviewed together until they say exactly what you mean.
  3. Sign & store — properly executed and witnessed, with clear guidance on where everything lives.
